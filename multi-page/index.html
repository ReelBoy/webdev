Welcome to Portland, Oregon

Nestled between the Willamette and Columbia rivers, Portland is a city of bridges, books, and boundless creativity. Known for its quirky charm, lush green spaces, and world-class food scene, the Pacific Northwest's biggest little city has something for everyone.

Why Portland?

I moved to Portland in 2015 and instantly fell in love with the city's unique blend of urban amenities and natural beauty. Here, you can spend the morning hiking in Forest Park, the afternoon browsing independent bookstores, and the evening enjoying some of the best farm-to-table dining in the country.

What makes Portland special isn't just its attractions—it's the people. There's a genuine sense of community here, whether you're at a farmers market, a neighborhood street fair, or just waiting for your coffee at a local cafe. Portlanders take pride in supporting local businesses and preserving the character of their distinct neighborhoods.

Quick Facts About Portland

  • Population: Approximately 650,000 (2.5 million metro area)
  • Nickname: Rose City / Stumptown / Bridgetown
  • Founded: 1845
  • Number of bridges: 12 within city limits
  • Annual rainfall: 43 inches
  • Number of food carts: 500+

🌟 Featured: Forest Park

One of the largest urban forests in the United States, Forest Park spans over 5,100 acres with more than 80 miles of trails. It's my favorite escape when I need to disconnect and recharge. The Wildwood Trail, which runs through the park, actually extends 30 miles through Portland's west hills!

Portland by the Seasons

Each season brings something unique to Portland. Spring explodes with cherry blossoms and the famous Portland Rose Festival. Summer offers perfect hiking weather and outdoor concerts. Fall brings crisp air and beautiful foliage in the parks. Winter? That's when you appreciate Portland's incredible coffee shops and cozy bookstores.
