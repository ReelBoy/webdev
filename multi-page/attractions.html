Must-See Attractions in Portland

After living in Portland for several years, these are my personal recommendations for visitors. From world-famous landmarks to local hidden gems, here's what you shouldn't miss.

Top 4 Portland Experiences

Powell's City of Books

Occupying a full city block, Powell's is the largest independent new and used bookstore in the world. They have over 1 million books across 3,500 different sections. The color-coded rooms help you navigate, but getting lost here is half the fun. Pro tip: ask for a map at the front desk and save your receipt—it's good for free parking validation!

Food Cart Pods

Portland's food cart culture is legendary. Unlike food trucks elsewhere, Portland carts gather in "pods"—clusters of 5-50 carts with shared seating. Cartopia downtown is open until 4am on weekends, and you can find everything from Korean-Mexican fusion to vegan Ethiopian food. My favorite pod is Prost Marketplace on Mississippi Avenue.

Forest Park

The Wildwood Trail through Forest Park is my go-to for weekend hikes. It connects to the Washington Park system, so you can hike all the way to the Oregon Zoo. The Pittock Mansion sits on the park's edge and offers the best view of Mount Hood over the city skyline.

Tom McCall Waterfront Park

This 30-acre park along the Willamette River is Portland's front yard. It hosts the Rose Festival, Blues Festival, and countless weekend events. Even when nothing's scheduled, it's perfect for walking, biking, or just watching the bridges rise and fall.

My Personal Recommendations

If you only have one day:

  1. Start at Powell's (opens 10am)
  2. Lunch at a food cart pod in the Pearl District
  3. Afternoon at the International Rose Test Garden
  4. Dinner in the Alberta Arts District
  5. Evening drinks at a rooftop bar with bridge views
